# Pesach 2026
<hr>

<p>
  Pesach 2026 was fun to say the least. I ended up waiting to long to sign up for any seders, and because I had assumed I would be going to a seder I didnt 
  actually prepare for a seder at home; meaning i didnt have enough rescources to actually do a seder either night. 
  However I DID make sure to stay away from chametz (leaven), ate at least for shmura matzoh both nights and drink the min 4 cups (in my case
  large sips from a jug of grape juice) both nights as well. Plus even though everything wasnt perfect, I still made sure to remember 
  the Exodus from egypt by 1) thinking about it a lot and 2) making sure to read the relevent chapters in my Chumash when that happened.
  Personally, I actually wish I had put more work, effort and planning into it. But hey, maybe HaShem will make it happen next year in Jerusalem!<br>
  I of course made sure to sell our Chametz before hand; Plus I went though the house with a candle and cleared our house of all Chametz, then I nullified and declared ownerless any I had missed. The night before Pesach<br>
  Plus I'm actually quite happy I got a non-observant family member on the phone while I searched with the candle and declared all the Chametz ownerless, withmy intention that it 
  apply to them as and any Chametz they had as well. <br>
  The next day, I wasnt really able to burn my Chametz, so I just made sure it was in the trash before hand. I DID however remind a friend to burn their Chametz, which they promptly did (to my amazement, as I was not expecting them to just set some stuff on fire like that lol)
  Overall I had a nice Pesach, both being somewhat happy I did not have to do Tefillin, but frankly really glad When I got back to doing it.
