# Times of prayer (at least what I do)

<hr>

So what are the times of prayer, and what do I do?<br>
Well, there are three basic times to pray daily: Morning (Shacharit), Afternoon (Mincha) and Evening (Ma'ariv). Generally, 
You say the Shema prayer twice daily, once before the main part of Shacharit the morning prayer, and once before the main part of Ma'ariv the evening prayer. <br>
For men, this is an obligation and for women it is a not nescissary, but it is appreciated. You must say each prayer at a set time detailed in something called the Zmanim which is basically a 
schedule for what time you do certain actions. It is an extremly exact and heavily calculated calender, so the times change a little bit every day throughout the year. 
There is generlly a specific time you can say the morning Shema prayer, with the last chance to pray the full Shacharit service generally 30-60 mins after (it all depends on your location!).<br>
Generally although you are technically allowed to say the Shema and evening prayer until dawn, it is a practiced instituted by the Sages to pray before legal midnight (not usually exactly 12 AM).
The Shema is quite important and is biblically mandated, but the Amida is he main part of all three prayers. The Amida is a section of 18 blessings drafted by the scribe Ezra from the Bible,
with the 19th being added later. Every Aminda is the same when prayed on regular weekdays that have no holidays and are not <i>Hol Hamoed</i> (yes weekdays legally also mean sunday, just not Shabbat). On any other kind of day 
like Shabbat or a holiday/Yom Tov, the Amida is different and may include/omit some things. It is best to look up the Amida for any specific holiday or event. Usually you say 
a prayer called the Aleinu as a kind of ending prayer. The Aleinu is not always the last prayer of the day, depending on the time of year (for example from Passover to Shavuot) you can say 
other things. Honestly, I am not as familliar with this, and I tend to follow along in my prayer book for instructions regarding these special days. My main point is thus:<br>
I generally say the Shema, Amida and Aleinu prayers in that order every day, and I think of it as the basics of what is required. There is more for sure (and 
depending on your custom the prayers may differ by service) and I intend to tell and explain as I learn. Thanks for reading!
