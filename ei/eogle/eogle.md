<hr>

First off, the language.<br>
This is a compound language, meaning that words can stack to create new meaning. <br>
The general concept in this language is when referring to an object or concept, you compound the words to make words in this language.<br>
Lets take an imaginary example: If you want to say 'A man is running' you would say every word, so you would say "(A) Man is running". But if you wanted to refer to the concept of a man running in general, you would say, according to the ruled of the language, "(A)Manisrunning".<br>
In this example of the structure of the language, to refer to a concept, regardless of how complex or specific it is, you use the compound form. If not, and you are refering to an acive action, you say/pronounce the words spaced out/individually.<br>

Ok; now to the language's words itself:<br>
This language has ten base words - yes thats right, only ten words. That means all concepts and other words are compounds of these original ten. <br>

So, lets say you wanted to name a baby boy. What would be a nice thing you want for him? Lets say you wish for him to be tall, so you would name him 'Tall'.<br>
How do you do that in this language? Well, you would take the root word 'W' (pronounced 'wuh') which means much, and you would/could combine it with the root word 'E' (pronounced 'eeh') which means Male/big. This would create the name 'We' (pronounced 'Wee') Meaning tall man. Now you could ask yourself, "Doesn't W also mean Action and E also mean
Yes/affirmitive, thus meaning 'Man who does action'?" Well the answer is yes, it does! In this language words can have several meanings, including double meanings. This creates fun lore, and essentually requires that 'you just had to be there'. Much of it relies on context.
That is why this page exists, to document the linguistic and cultural history of the world of EO: G. <br>
Something to keep in mind is that places are named after people and things, and often derive roots from these names. Therefore, you can start to simulate regional culture and sub cultures, allowing for extremly complex societies and worlds. <br>
To illistrate this pount, in the real world there was, according to Greek myth a king named Tros. He founded the city of Troy, which the Greeks besieged, famously as a last ditch effort creating the Trojan horse.
