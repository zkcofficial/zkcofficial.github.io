# Ethnographic Odyssy: Genesis Cycle two
<hr>

### This game is called EO: Genesis Cycle two because this is the first book in the story of Ethnographic Odyssy, a game which follows the affairs of the world, who rules over it, and what the cultures and languages of these people adhere to.


<hr>

Part I (title) <br>
<p>
With the growing economic power of the combines dutchees of Eseae & Ium, known as the house of "Eseaewium", it's duke Eihimhea'Eseaewium'e (Eihimhea for short) needed to be married, and more importantly he wanted to have a stronger grip on the major trade routes, so he arranged for the marriage of himself to the princess sister of duke Eboe'Eboe'e, ruler of the dutchee We'aebm. With this marriage, he also made a deal with duke Eboe'Eboe'e to marry their future firstborn son to the duke's toddler firstborn daughter, thus formally inheriting the the combined powers of Ium, Eseae and We'aebm and uniting the houses of Eseaewium and Eboe.</p>

The king of Seae king Weem, made a deal with duke Eihimhea to allow this union and provide military protection for Eseaewium and their partners. In exchange, Eihimhea will lower prices of the products manufactured by his dutchee Ium which the kingdom Seae depends on, and he will lower the taxes which Seae & Easeae traders must pay for using the roads which run through Eseae. The duke of We'aebm, duke Eboe'Eboe'e, agreed that his domain which is between the major producing powers of the kingdoms Seae and We would forgoe over half the taxes due to them from Seae traders to bolster the deal. <br>
In order to afford this however, the duke Eboe'Eboe'e offset the taxes due from the Seae traders to the We traders. This offset was over 50%, meaning the prices of all imported goods and rescources went up one-third for the kingdom of We. This obviously angered the king of We, king We'o.

<p>
  The king of We, We'o decided to stop this union by any means nesecairy, deciding on a plan to assisinate the king of Seae, Weem, and the bride to be princess sister Iuhwehe
</p>
