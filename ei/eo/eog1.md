Part 2: Rebuilding from the rubble<br>
With new rulers in place and the experiance from previous wars faught by their ancestors, many of the kings sought to foster peace between their lands. 
<p>
One of these kings in particular, king Im'ea of Seae, was keen to improve relations with his former enemy, king Eiseae of the kingdom Eiseae. Through messengers, king Im'ea managed to somewhat smooth relations with king Eiseae, with the two ultimatly deciding that the trade of rescources between the two kingdoms was benificial. The kingdom Eiseae provided iron and copper ores, plus a large supply of salmon caught from the resource rich Wuu river. This trade flowed south into the metropolotain center of the kingdom Seae, where traders and elites from many lands would congregate. </p>
<p>
Trade also flowed north from Seae to Eiseae, with a somewhat smaller output of salmon from the old Wuh river as their main export. It is worth noting that the city Seae wasn't as wealthy in exports as it's neighbors, but it was a multi-national hub, where people from across the world congregated. This caused money and rescources to flow into the city, as it was a party hub and opprotunities were ample. </p>
<p>
The next thing to happen was king Um'e of the kingdom of Um saw the new trade and economic opprotunities that came with prolonged contact with outside nations. See, king Um'e saw his father, king Um keep the country out of war by adopting an isolationist policy, heavily restricting the movement of his citizens and outside nation's citizens, nearly shutting the country off entirly. This strategy worked well in a lifetime of king Um, when war and bitterness was frequent, but with new times came a new political climate, one which would favor a lift on the isolationist policy in place for so many years. So king Um'e, while still reluctant, lifted most parts of the ban, letting trade flow without revealing to much of the country's secrecy. This had massive ripple effects, as it flooded the already strong Seae-Eiseae market and trade routes with new merchandise and a flood of traders, socialites and elites from Um. The result was the city of Seae became even more of a social and trade hub, being between the kingdoms of Um and Eiseae. The kingdom of Um would make rare purple cloth made from thread only spun in the marshes and Greyfish only caught on the 'Uiwu' river, trading these rescources far north, all the way to the northern highlands of Eiseae. </p> 
<p>
Although king Um'e had a legitimate heir, prince Um'i, he also had an illigitimate daughter, princess Ibm'Umhe. He gave her a small vassel state south called "Ium" to ensure she was provided for, letting her become "Dutchess Ibm'Umhe of Ium".</p>
<p>
Finally, there was the kingdom of We, ruled by co-monarches king We'e and king U'We. They, due to distrust among each other and a reluctance to trade with their brother king Im'ea of Seae out of the shame of not inheriting entire realms of their own, were slow to trade. Although officially they were on good terms with king Im'ea, they held quite a lot of mistrust, not letting the potential of their economy be d. They only sent pre-approved traders and dignitaries, often with the purpose of spying on the city Seae and the various other nations who lived there. This has often given travelers from We a bad reputation for being sneaky and untrustworthy.</p>
<p>
Yet a tragegy occured: To king We'e, a daughter was born by the name of "Iu'Wehe". This was unacceptable and dangerous in the capitol, seeing as she was the second born, her father king We'e thought she had potential to be the reason for court and civil strife if she were to remain in the kingdom, so in an unpresidented move he had her sent away to be raised by her uncle, king Im'ea of Seae under the new name of "Mimhea'we". King Im'ea, due to the wealth of his city Seae, he decided to create a small state to the north-west of Seae called "Eseae" for his adoptive daughter Mimhea'we, letting her become "Lady Mimhea'we of Eseae".</p>
<p>
Due to the inheritance laws of the kingdom of Eiseae, each heir had a claim to the throne. There were three heirs, so they were expected to fight over the thrown. However, the king Im'ea had a solution. This solution was to give the hand of his adopted daughter in marrige to prince Ea'Eiseae of Eiseae, so that he could have land and a title in the dominion of King Im'ea, and not have to fight over his father's thrown back home. This worked, making the prince of Eiseae the Lord of Eseae. </p>
<p>
With the departure of one of the claimants to the Eiseae thrown, many people in the kingdom sided with the youngest son of the king, Ei'Eiseae. This left the second born prince, Ee'Eiseae with few supporters and a high pressure to leave the country on threat of violent defeat by his brother's forces. So, seeing the situation unfold, king Um'e decided to take after king Im'ea and invite the prince to his kingdom and give him his daughter's hand in marrige as well, so as to let thr prince inherit the state of "Ium" and be under the influence of king Um'e. He invited the prince Ee'Eiseae to have his daughter's hand in marrige, and it worked out well, with prince Ee'Eiseae becoming Duke Ee'Eiseae of Ium through marrige.</p>
<p>
In the year 140, all the kings of the new generation had died. In their stead the princes of the world rose up and accepted their new positions. These princes were:<br>
Prince U'Wehe son of co-monarch king U'We. He rose and accepted his father's role as co-monarch. 
The next prince was prince We'i, son of co-monarch king We'e. He also rose and accepted the role of co-monarch king.<br>
The next prince was prince Um'i son of king Um'e of the kingdom Um. He rose and took the mantle of his father, becoming king Um'i of Um. </p>
<p>
Prince Ee'Eiseae of Eiseae married Dutchess Ibm'Umhe of Ium, becoming Duke Ee'Eiseae of Ium.</p>
<p>
The next was prince Imhea'e, who inherited the title of his father king Im'ea of Seae, becoming king Imhea'e of Seae.<br>
Prince Ea'Eiseae of Eiseae married Lady Mimhea'we of Eseae, becoming Lord Ea'Eiseae of Eseae.</p>
<p>
The last was prince Ei'Eiseae third born son of king Eiseae of Eiseae. He inherited his father's role as king Ei'Eiseae of Eiseae. </p>

Part 3.<br>
<p>
The king Imhea'e of Seae wanted to unite his realm with that of his sub dominion Eseae, which he didn't technically own. He wanted his son, Imhea'i to inherit both his own personal domain of Seae and his vassel, the dutchy of Eseae. To do this, Imhea'e struck a deal with Mimhea'We dutchess of Eseae to have his son Imhea'i marry her daughter Uwi, thus uniting both domains under one owner. There was one problem though, Uwi was the second born child of dutchess Mimhea'We, meaning she would not by default inherit the dutchy, This would go to her older brother Ei'Imhea. So king Imhea'e devised a plan: He knew that Ei'Imhea was incredibly reclusive and eccentric, and not overly gifted in ruling, preferring to be alone than be in the company of the court. So king Imhea'e gave Ei'Imheae an altimatum: leave and marry a foreign princess, giving up your inheritance of Eseae and letting your sister Uwi have it, or I will use your eccentric nature as evidence of your incompetance and innability to rule due to your mental state, using that as an excuse to have you imprisoned here. <br>
Sufficed to say, Ei'Imhea understood the threat and agreed to marry a foreign princess and gain dual control of her realm, giving up his inheritance of Eseae to his sister, Uwi. </p>

<p>
Meanwhile, in the kingdom of We, the two dual monarchs, brothers We'i and U'Wehe both see a problem: They both have sons, and both sides of the family could potentially claim the thrown. So in order to maintain peace and prevent any poential future strife, they hatch a plan: King We'i and his line will stay king over the throne, and his brother U'Wehe will march with his sons U'Wehe'e and Weem on the kingdom Seae, deposing its rulers and installing themselves as the rulers of the land. Thus, they will abolish the two king system in We, making king We'i and his descendants sole rulers over the kingdom of We and U'Wehe and his descendants sole rulers over the kingdom of Seae.</p>
